Penguins are flightless birds that live mostly in the Southern Hemisphere. They are well adapted to cold environments and use their strong flippers to swim through the water. Penguins often live in large groups called colonies, where they help protect one another from danger.

These birds spend much of their lives in the ocean searching for food such as fish, krill, and squid. On land, they move by waddling, sliding on their bellies, or standing together to stay warm. Penguins are known for their caring behavior, especially when parents take turns looking after their eggs and chicks.
